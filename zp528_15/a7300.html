Dried wood ear (Kikurage) from Yokote City, Akita Prefecture, Japan

Wood ear (Kikurage) is a kind of mushroom. Most of the Kikurage are imported products, and the distribution volume of Japanese domestic products is only about 5%.
One of the rare domestic wood ear producers is the “Takahashi Farm” from Yokote City, Akita Prefecture. Their Kikurage is overwhelmingly larger and thicker than imported ones.

Kikurage is extremely nutritious and has top-class nutrition among mushrooms.
Its nutritional value is also used as a material for herbal medicine, and it is rich in iron, vitamin D, and dietary fiber, and is low in calories. You can expect various health effects such as recovery from fatigue, anti-aging, prevention of diabetes, and prevention of osteoporosis.

Kikurage from the Takahashi farm is a very large palm-sized one.
It goes well with Chinese dishes of course, also can be shredded and mixed with salad.
