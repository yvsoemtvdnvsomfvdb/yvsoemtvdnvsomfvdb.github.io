庵地焼（あんちやき）は、新潟の大河・阿賀野川の中流域の阿賀野市保田、通称庵地（あんち）地区で焼かれている陶器です。
「庵地の黒」といわれるほど、黒色の釉薬に特徴があります。
元々は江戸時代から続く“保田焼”が前身で、同地には昭和の初期、様々な諸窯がありましたが、旗野窯を除く各窯は第二次世界大戦を前後に絶えました。
旗野窯は明治11年創業。民芸陶器としての130年の歴史を先代から引き継ぎ、3代目の旗野義山（義夫）が研鑽努力し民芸窯としての地歩を固めました。現在では、三姉妹が継ぎ今も土作りからすべての工程を手造りで行っています。

Anchiyaki is a type of pottery fired in Yasuda, Agano City, commonly known as the Anchi district, located in the middle reaches of the Agano River, a large river in Niigata.
The black glaze is so distinctive that it is called "the black of the Anti"
Its predecessor was Yasuda ware, which has been around since the Edo period, and there were various kilns in the area in the early Showa period, but all of the kilns except Hatano kiln ceased to exist around World War II. I did.
Hatano kiln was founded in 1891. Inheriting the 130-year history of folk pottery from the previous generation, the third generation Yoshiyama Hatano (Yoshio) has worked hard to improve and solidify its position as a folk pottery. Nowadays, three sisters continue to handcraft every step from preparing the soil.
