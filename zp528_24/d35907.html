Bento is said to have originated as a way to eat on the way home from hunting or farming so that people didn"t have to go back home. From 1573 to 1603, it seems that the custom of eating bento during cherry blossom viewing and tea ceremonies began. Since then, the use has changed with the times, such as makunouchi bento, ekiben, and commuting to work or school, but it is still an indispensable tool and culture for Japanese people.
